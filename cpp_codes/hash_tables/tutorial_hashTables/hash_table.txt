Learning about hash tables

Has:
	-key
	-value

A way of implementing an associative array.
Write a Hash function which looks at a key, evaluates it, and give
us an index.
Ex:                           0   1   2   3   4
	Hash(key)->index        | T | X | W | O | K |
  O	Hash(Paul)-> 3 -----		
  X Hash(Person1)-> 1	   |
  T Hash(Person2)-> 0	   |
  W Hash(Person3)-> 2 	   |
  K Hash(Person4)-> 4	   | -> Collision since indexes match!
			   |
  What if we put:	   |
  Z	Hash(Person)-> 3 ---

  So we use a linked list:
    0   1   2   3   4
  | T | X | W | O | K |     If we have a collision, then we 
		|           can link it onto a list
	      | Z |
